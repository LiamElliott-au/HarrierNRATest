Privacy Policy - New Zealand

1
NATIONAL ROADSIDE ASSIST RESPECTS YOUR PRIVACY
1.1
National Roadside Assist Pty Ltd and Harrier National New Zealand Limited (together “NRA”) respects your right to privacy. This Privacy Policy (the “Policy”) sets out NRA’s policy in relation to information provided by, or in connection with, customers and inquiring customers (excluding trade customers).
1.2
The Policy explains how in New Zealand NRA, and any motor vehicle dealers (“the Dealer”) providing products administered by NRA (National Roadside Assist), will obtain, store and use information about or in connection with customers, their vehicles, and goods and services provided or contemplated, including “personal information” as defined in the Privacy Act 1993 (“the Privacy Act”).
1.3
The rights and obligations of NRA in respect of your privacy under this Policy, will also apply to the Dealer that is responsible for selling, or discussing with you, a NRA product or service (in addition to any Privacy Policy they may have).
1.4
If privacy issues arise in relation to a NRA administered product or service, the terms of this policy prevail for the purposes of the handling of your private information in connection with the NRA administered service. However, the Dealer may have other privacy or information policies which apply for other purposes.
1.5
Please read and understand the contents of this Policy before authorising its terms by signing the Mechanical Protection Plan Agreement under the heading “Privacy Policy: collecting your personal information”.

3
HOW WE COLLECT YOUR PERSONAL INFORMATION
3.1
We collect personal information from you in a variety of ways, including: when you interact with us electronically or in person; when you access our website; and when we provide our goods/services to you.
3.2
When you (or others on your behalf) provide NRA and/or a Dealer providing a NRA branded product with any information about you, your vehicle, associated goods or services, or related information, in connection with an inquiry or transaction, NRA and the Dealer will assume that, unless advised otherwise by you (or someone on your behalf), that you are at least authorising NRA and the Dealer (and other related parties as specified in this Policy) to:
 •
retain that information for the purposes of dealing with, and as part of the business record of, the current inquiry transaction and any future transactions; and
 •
retain information provided or obtained in the context of a sale, purchase, service, parts, repairs or related transaction as business records, and for the purposes of any subsequent sale/purchase, service, parts or repair transaction in relation to your vehicle(s), including the inquiry/transaction involved, and for any lawful statutory or regulatory purpose, including responding to any inquiry by a government agency (where NRA or the Dealer is lawfully obliged to do so); and
 •
share that information between the Dealer and NRA (as administrator of any relevant Mechanical Protection Plan or between NRA and the relevant Dealers); and
 •
share that information with any other entity that is to be involved in providing services associated with your vehicle or as otherwise identified in this Policy (but only for those purposes or as a business record relevant to those purposes).
4
USE OF YOUR PERSONAL INFORMATION
4.1
We use your information to provide our service to you. We also use it to improve our service and to notify you of opportunities that we think you might be interested in.
4.2
We do not provide your information to third parties, except that we may provide your information to our business partners who assist us in the provision of our services to you, for the purposes of assisting you with any obligations arising in relation to those services.
4.3
NRA work with companies that help NRA to run their business. These companies provide services such as delivering customer support, processing credit card payments, and sending emails on our behalf. In some cases, these companies have access to some of your personal information in order to provide services to you on our behalf. They are not permitted to use your information for their own purposes.
4.4
NRA may also share your personal information:
(a)
When you agreed to the sharing.
(b)
Within the NRA family of companies for purposes described in this privacy policy.
(c)
To diagnose or fix technology problems.
(d)
With our resellers and other sales partners if you are a business customer and agreed to receive NRA marketing communications. Note that we do not share personal information about consumers with third parties for their own marketing purposes unless the consumer agreed to that sharing.
 
 
4.4
(e)
When we are lawfully required to provide information in response to a subpoena, court order, or other applicable law or legal process.
(f)
When we have a good faith belief that the disclosure is necessary to prevent or respond to fraud, defend our websites or applications against attacks, or protect the property and safety of NRA, our customers and users, or the public.
(g)
If we merge with or are acquired by another company, we sell the NRA website, application, or business unit or all or a substantial portion of our assets are acquired by another company. In those cases your information will likely be one of the assets that is transferred.
(h)
With any other dealers and/or mechanics responsible for servicing or repairing your vehicle (in the event that the Dealer who sold you the MPP is unable to) for the purposes of delivering the warranty service provided for under the MPP, and in other circumstances where required for the terms and conditions of the MPP Agreement to be fulfilled.
4.5
We may share or publish aggregate information that doesn’t specifically identify you, such as statistical information about visitors to our websites or statistical information about how customers use our applications and hosted services.
4.6
NRA shall retain your personal information for the purposes of the Mechanical Protection Plan (including the information outlined in 2.1 above) after the expiry of your cover under a Mechanical Protection Plan as business records of the arrangement unless and to the extent required by law.
5
LOG FILE INFORMATION
5.1
Log file information is automatically reported by your browser each time you make a request to access (i.e., visit) a web page or app. It can also be provided when the content of the webpage or app is downloaded to your browser or device.
5.2
When you use our Service, our servers automatically record certain log file information, including your web request, Internet Protocol ("IP") address, browser type, referring / exit pages and URLs, number of clicks and how you interact with links on the Service, domain names, landing pages, pages viewed, and other such information. We may also collect similar information from emails sent to our Users which then help us track which emails are opened and which links are clicked by recipients. The information allows for more accurate reporting and improvement of the Service.
6
SECURITY OF YOUR PERSONAL INFORMATION
6.1
We take reasonable steps to protect your personal information. However we are not liable to the maximum extent permissible by law, for any unauthorised access to this information.
7
ACCESS TO YOUR PERSONAL INFORMATION
7.1
You are entitled, (pursuant to principle 6 of the Privacy Act) to access personal information we hold and, (pursuant to Principle 7 of the Privacy Act) to correct that information. You can do so by contacting us on privacy@nationalroadsideassist.com.au.
8
COMPLAINTS ABOUT PRIVACY
8.1
If you have any complaints about our privacy practices, please feel free to send in details of your complaints to privacy@nationalroadsideassist.com.au.
8.2
We take complaints very seriously and will respond promptly once we have received written notice of your complaint. If we cannot resolve the complaint to your satisfaction within a reasonable time, you or we may refer the complaint to the Privacy Commissioner.
